Salmon Onigiri

Description

Onigiri are among Japanese oldest foods, eaten since at least the 11th century. Simple and portable design has kept them popular to these days.

Onigiri are considered everyday food. They are common for lunches, picnics, travel. In Japan, convenience stores stock them extensively for busy commuters, students and workers.

Ingredients

  • 1 fillet of salmon (180g-200g)
  • 2 cups water
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • 1 tbsp of roasted sesame seeds
  • 5-6 handfuls of rice (500-600g)
  • 2 full-sized sheet of Nori seaweed paper (cut lengthwise into thirds)

Steps

  1. To make the poached salmon, sprinkle some salt onto the salmon fillet. Put two cups of water into a shallow pan and bring to the boil.
  2. Place the salmon into the pan. Put the lid on, simmer for 10-15 minutes. Make sure the salmon is cooked through. Remove from the pot, pat dry with paper towel or cloth.
  3. Flake the salmon. Mix 2 tbsp salmon, sesame seeds, and a handful of cooked Japanese rice in a bowl.
  4. Cut a full-sized sheet of Nori seaweed paper into thirds lengthwise.
  5. Wet both hands with water to avoid the rice sticking to them.
  6. Put a pinch of salt on your hands, rub them together to spread the salt evenly.
  7. Place a handful (90-100g) of cooked salmon rice into one hand.
  8. Add a teaspoon of tuna mayo on the centre of the rice. Cover with rice, make it gradually into a triangular shape. Make sure the onigiri does not fall apart.
  9. Wrap the onigiri with nori.
  10. Place a bit of the filling on top of the onigiri as a garnish if you want.
